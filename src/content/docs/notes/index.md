Field notes are the dated essays on this site. Each one records what was built, measured, or believed on the date at the top of the page. The [reference](/reference/) pages are kept current as tools and prices change. Field notes are not. Each one is a record of a specific moment, and that is deliberate.

Opinion is welcome here in a way it is not elsewhere on the site. One rule still applies: every claim comes from a system that actually shipped and ran, and every number traces to a real measurement or a published report.

When a conclusion changes, the old note is not quietly edited. A new note says what changed and links back, and the old note stands as an honest record of its date. Read every note with its date in mind.
